The architects decision to use clear glass and to splash only a few precise places with touches of colour corresponds to his wish to keep a tight rein on the use of light  a primordial element in the definition of the interior volumes. The interior atmosphere of a space affects us greatly, and if we dont get enough light, we fall ill.

The use of a small amount of colour can also help to create a sense of depth, drawing the eye to specific areas of the room and creating a visual flow that guides the observer through the space. In this case, the pops of colour are strategically placed to create a sense of warmth and invitingness, while the abundance of clear glass allows the light to flood in and illuminate the entire space. This balance of light and colour creates a sense of harmony and balance in the room, allowing the occupant to feel calm and relaxed.
