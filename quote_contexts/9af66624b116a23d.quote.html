In discussing the character of ornament, in order to then debate a sense of an operative digital ornament, I will start by discussing the surface, as both the primary substrate or even condition for ornamentation, as well as a characterisation that might be perceived onto ornament superficial, but which I will try to surpass and turn into operation. So, starting with the ornamental surface, or the superficial ornament.

In considering the ornamental surface, we find it already marked by the presence of ornament, as if the very surface itself has become a site of ornamentation, and thus the question arises as to whether the surface can be seen as ornament, or if it is merely the condition for ornament to operate. The surface, in other words, appears to be both the foundation and the ornament itself, a tension that will be crucial to my argument regarding the operative digital ornament.
