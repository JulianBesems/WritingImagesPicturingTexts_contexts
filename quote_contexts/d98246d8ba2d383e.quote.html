Currently, this style of figurative ornamentation is widely seen as superficial, making the surface not just substrate but characterisation of the ornament. However, if we look beyond the figurative, and to the role of these subjects they also acted as indices of communication, expressing the nature of the building to which they were attached.

These indices of communication have the potential to transcend their physical locations, serving as a form of visual language that can convey meaning and significance beyond the immediate context. In this sense, the ornamentation becomes a key to understanding the cultural and historical context of the building, providing a window into the past. As such, the superficiality of the ornamentation is revealed to be a façade, hiding a deeper significance that is waiting to be uncovered.
