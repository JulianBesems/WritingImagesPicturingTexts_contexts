The interplay between light and structure is a crucial aspect of architectural design, as it not only affects the functionality of a space but also its aesthetic appeal. The way light interacts with a structure can create a sense of drama, highlighting certain features and creating a sense of depth. In addition, the use of light can also be used to create a sense of ambiguity, with shadows and highlights blurring the distinction between different elements. In this sense, light and structure can be seen as two sides of the same coin, working together to create a cohesive and effective design.
