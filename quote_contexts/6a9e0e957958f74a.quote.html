In all of this the window serves as the prop for discovering new and unexpected relationships between the inside and outside. This window, like any other, relies on mechanisms of operation, instruments of opening and closure.

In this way, the window becomes a threshold, a liminal space that blurs the boundaries between the two. Through its glassy surface, the outside world is refracted, filtered, and distorted, as if it were a dream or a hallucination. The view, in turn, is framed by the window's edges, which slice through the visual field like a pair of scissors.
