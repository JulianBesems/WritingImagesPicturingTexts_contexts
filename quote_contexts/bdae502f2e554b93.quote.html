3.6 INTERACTIVITY "The new new scientific spirit has rediscovered the art of Penelope: while the sailor comes and goes, approaches and moves away, she weaves and unwinds the map of the journey, the portulan of the universe. - Serres, Le Systme de Leibniz et Ses Modles Mathmatiques. Following this statement by Serres,the modern scientific attitude shifts from Ariadnes thread to Penelopes weaving: from an objective singular notion to an operative stance. In our case, now that the elements and projections of the WIPT editor are in place, this section turns to use: how writing interactively negotiates across the domains of the multi-modal cast in practice. Rather than a single deductive chain, interaction unfolds as a woven network of concurrent entries and crossings, more tapestry than thread, so that its cast is always oriented to the whole it approaches through successive correspondences.

The practice of interactive writing thus involves a continuous process of reweaving and reorienting the network, as each new entry or correspondence is added. It is a dynamic negotiation between the writer's intention, the reader's understanding, and the emergent meaning that arises from their interactions. By embracing this complexity, writers can create texts that are at once more responsive, more adaptive, and more generative than traditional written works.
