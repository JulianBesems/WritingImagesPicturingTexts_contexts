Leontes sees, as we have begun to see, that the instability of meaning and uncertainty of reference he is experiencing first-hand—what I have generally termed linguistic indeterminacy —is not simply a f function of expression but of interpretation as well. It arises, that is, not only out of an imperfection in the medium or the speaker’s use of it, but out of the radical subjectivity of the listener or interpreter. For this reason, it is doubly inescapable, a condition that prevents us from ever arriving at certain or complete understanding in human affairs.[Hartman, Geoffrey H., and Patricia A Parker *Shakespeare and the Question of Theory*. New edition. 1986.]
