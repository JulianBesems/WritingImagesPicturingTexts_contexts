And to demonstrate the possibility of making these projections specific, personal and curated. The datasets that will establish these multi-modal casts are thus chosen to be relatively small, and of a personal character. In rationale these selections are therefore opposed to that of the PUBLIC datasets, where generality and broadness were searched for in those, we approach these datasets from a personal point of view, or at least with the intent of them being of a particular quality or clear bias. FILM The first type of documents selected for the multi-modal casts are films with subtitles. These provide a naturally occurring multi-modal corpus: a juxtaposition of image and narrative text that is co-present but non-equivalent. Here, each frame articulates visual tone and scenographic presence, while subtitles render the spoken narrative. Crucially, only non-descriptive subtitle tracks are used to foreground narrative dialogue, eliminating external commentary or auditory cues. In total the film collection used throughout the thesis consists of 215 films, which provides the multi-modal cast used throughout the majority of the chapters. In the following chapters we will sometimes also work with smaller subsets, for instance by only taking films by one director e.g. Tarantino or studio e.g. Ghibli. The overall collection is the product of my personal taste in films, primarily comprised of films or series I have seen and enjoyed, with some films I've been meaning to watch for a long time but not had the chance yet. The choices also rest on a certain sense of aesthetic quality as well as historic relevance that I would ascribe to this collection, however this is of course a completely personal taste. The point is that it forms a sensibility which is not purely generic and through this allows for a tuning between the text and image domains that is particular to this personal taste. Acknowledging that another person would tune it differently, and thus establish a proportionality between the two domains which is distinct from this one.

We will often refer to this film collection as the PERSONALPRIVATE dataset, in distinction to the PUBLIC datasets that are not curated from a personal perspective. By doing so, we aim to make explicit the differences in approach between these two types of datasets and to highlight the significance of personal curation in shaping the multi-modal casts that inform our argument.
