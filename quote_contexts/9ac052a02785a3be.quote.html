To misread this layer as merely decorative is to strip it of speech. Although the aforementioned subjects of ornament are easily dismissed as kitsch from a contemporary view, the objects made their surface legible, not dissimilar to pictograms:

...as if the hand of the maker, in the act of creation, had inscribed meaning upon the material, rendering it no longer static, but rather a dynamic, interactive entity.  The more one gazed upon these objects, the more the surface seemed to shift, like the slow unwinding of a thread, revealing hidden patterns and narratives.  In the end, it was not the object itself, but the act of interpretation that imbued it with significance.
