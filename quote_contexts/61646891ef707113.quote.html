In all of this the window serves as the prop for discovering new and unexpected relationships between the inside and outside. This window, like any other, relies on mechanisms of operation, instruments of opening and closure. Fundamentally, glass is hard, yet fragile and brittle. An ingenious device to siphon daylight deeply into a building, this glazed opening serves also to share illumination between rooms demanding acoustic separation, so as to spread light in a peaceful way, free of disrupting noise.

The play of light on the window's surface, refracting the colors of the outside world, creates a dynamic interplay of contrasts and nuances that can evoke the passage of time, the fleeting nature of life, and the beauty of the world around us. In this way, the window serves not only as a portal to the outside, but also as a symbol of the ever-changing nature of our experiences and perceptions. The soft glow of the lamp in the room, casting shadows on the walls, serves as a counterpoint to the vibrant colors of the outside world, highlighting the duality of light and darkness that is inherent in our lives. As the sun sets, casting a warm orange glow over the room, we are reminded of the cyclical nature of time, and the way in which the world around us is constantly evolving and changing.
