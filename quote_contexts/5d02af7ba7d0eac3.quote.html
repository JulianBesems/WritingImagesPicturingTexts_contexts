Bühlmann’s reconstruction of Serres helps to fix the propositional stance. For Serres, models are not primarily representative or explanatory, but instrumental and operative: they “realise” a phenomenon and are themselves active, endowed with a kind of mentality or character. Models are not mirrors but devices: they create conditions under which relations can appear, be measured, and be transformed. Measurement still matters, but it may run along axes of probabilities, proximities, and indices, rather than along classical notions of correctness or exactitude. The ambition pursued here is to treat digital models in precisely this way: as architectonic instruments that facilitate reading, and re-reading, relations between heterogeneous communications.
