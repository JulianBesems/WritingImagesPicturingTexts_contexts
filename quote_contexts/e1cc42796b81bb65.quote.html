What is assembled in this thesis and its instrument WIPTe is, first of all, an occasion for conversation; an attempt to let multimodal machine learning become discussable from within architectural method, rather than treated as a foreign technical object that architecture merely applies. The contribution

This allows for a reevaluation of the role of data in architectural design, as well as the potential for machine learning to augment and support the design process. By bringing together the theoretical frameworks of architectural method and machine learning, WIPTe offers a unique perspective on the relationship between human and algorithmic agency in design.
