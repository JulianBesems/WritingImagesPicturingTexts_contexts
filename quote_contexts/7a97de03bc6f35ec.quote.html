The meaning of the wall is just as diverse as the uses of vertical surface can be, but there are at least two essential functions: providing structure and dividing space. The two can be separated, and thus the category of 'wall' divides into two: the bearing wall, separating roof from ground; and the partition wall, organizing movement within the resulting container. The former, it would seem, is as stable as the human need for shelter; the latter as changeable as our forms of sociability.
