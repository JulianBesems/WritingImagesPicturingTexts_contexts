Rather than modelling singular forms, the architectural task becomes one of filtering, relating, and projecting from within this excess. This emphasizes the design of relations and indices over objects and forms, pushing architectural thinking toward algebraic, procedural operations embedded in networks of meaning. Within such a framework, digital architectonics shifts from representation to modulation, engaging the "big plenty" not as overwhelming, but as the generative substrate of architectural thought.
