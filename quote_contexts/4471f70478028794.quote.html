In discussing the character of ornament, and to then suggest an operative sense of digital ornament, I begin from the surface, as both the primary substrate or even condition for ornamentation. Ornament embellishes; it is what meets the eye: pattern, colour, relief. Therefore, at first it presents at surface-level.

Ornament is often relegated to the realm of the superficial, where it is seen as mere decoration, lacking depth or substance. Yet, in the context of digital ornament, this superficiality is precisely what allows it to operate as a means of communication, as a way to convey meaning and significance. Moreover, the surface-level presentation of ornament raises questions about the relationship between the digital and the physical, and the ways in which these two realms intersect.
