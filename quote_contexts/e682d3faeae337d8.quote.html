This reflection, posed by doubleNegatives Architecture after echoing Wittgenstein’s famous assertion "The limits of my language mean the limits of my world", opens a critical terrain for this paper. If the limits of an architect’s digital language are shifting toward the geometric, centred around the 3D model, then we must ask what modes of architectural thinking are being excluded. This is not merely a philosophical issue; it is a practical and epistemic concern. What are the consequences when the qualitative, the speculative, and the non-geometric dimensions of architecture lose their footing in computational discourse?
