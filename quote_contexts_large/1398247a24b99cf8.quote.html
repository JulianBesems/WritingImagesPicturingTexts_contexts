1.2.2 PROPOSAL Given the scope, and the intent of engaging with methodology over architectural material, the point being made is epistemic: architecture is mediated by many modes that do not collapse into a single model. Architectural thought does not, and never did, reside in one privileged representation, but in the proportional negotiations between several - drawings, images, texts, models - each standing apart, yet held together in practice.

In this sense, architecture's complexity arises from the intricate web of representations that constitute its praxis. The discipline's heterogeneity is not a weakness, but rather a strength, as it allows for a multiplicity of approaches and perspectives to coexist and inform one another. By embracing this complexity, architects can cultivate a more nuanced and responsive understanding of the built environment.
