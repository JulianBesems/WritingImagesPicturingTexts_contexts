. . the floor. Thats right. The floor. Its typically the largest horizontal surface in the room and undeniably the most used. To provide a flat, horizontal surface on which desired human activities can take place, all buildings contain at least one floor. The walls are a large plane.  The sofa is a large plane.  The floor coverings and floor are large planes. ALL you need is a plane and a nice clear piece of wood. For example, a marble runner within a wooden floor could form a pathway through a hall and into a living space, perhaps continuing to the fireplace or window; That said, it should be remembered that arbitrarily eliminating as many interior walls as possible will not necessarily result in a better space.

As the walls begin to blur together, the room's layout becomes a jigsaw puzzle of planes and spaces, with the floor serving as the common denominator. In this sense, the floor can be seen as a unifying force, binding together disparate elements to create a cohesive whole. It is the foundation upon which all other design elements are built, and as such, it plays a crucial role in the overall aesthetic of the space.
