The figurative domain is addressed primarily through word labels. These are indices, anchors by which images can be grasped, not to be read as exhaustive descriptions of image content. Because the used vocabulary is dictated by the multimodal cast, the anchors are specific to that sensibility, and the figurative words can therefore be interpreted as a set of distinct symbolic tags rather than interpreting the words in their general textual meaning. The semantic identity of a label in the figurative domain is not assumed to match its textual counterpart; the two are related only architectonically, through the multimodal model as cast.
