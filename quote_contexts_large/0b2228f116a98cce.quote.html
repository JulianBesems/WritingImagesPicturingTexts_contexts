Fascinatingly, in the built environment, I wouldn't say we should expect or if you'd ask me hope for a return of obvious decorative ornament per-se. But there is something where the technology becomes ornament, in the sense that it conveys grandure, or hosts spectacle.

The gleaming skyscrapers of a distant metropolis, their reflective surfaces mirroring the vibrant city life below, are a testament to this concept. The intricate networks of pipes, wires, and ducts that crisscross beneath our feet, though often hidden from view, are a more humble yet no less impressive manifestation of this idea. In both cases, technology becomes an integral part of the urban landscape, shaping our experience of the city and its inhabitants.
