3.2 DOMAINS Domains in this context are the fields of information between which the projections take place, as well as those that establish said projections. In the architectural communication, domains could be understood as the ichnographic, orthographic and scenographic drawings. Here, given the focus on images and text, these will form the primary domains, with the third being a collection of documents that inherently contains both. The choice was made to have the interactions take place between two domains that can be seen as the generic, big enough to not be curated, and broad enough to be able to expose an exhaustive amount of information hosted by the writing. The projections between these two generics are however of a more precise nature, although we will explore the variety of scale within this as well.

These projections can be seen as filters that narrow down the information within the domains, creating a new, more focused domain, and allowing for the exploration of different aspects within the data. This new domain can be seen as a refinement of the initial domains, as it selects specific subsets of information within the larger domains. By doing so, it enables the writer to create a narrative that is more specific, yet still broad enough to encompass the complexities of the information at hand.
